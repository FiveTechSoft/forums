Agentic Employee - Empleado agéntico
Posted: Wed Feb 25, 2026 11:44 PM

An agentic employee (sometimes called a "digital employee," "AI employee," or "employee agent") refers to an autonomous AI system designed to function like a human worker inside an organization.

It's not a human person — it's an advanced **AI agent** that:

  • - Receives goals or tasks

  • - Reasons, plans, and breaks them down into steps
  • - Uses tools, accesses systems/APIs, searches for information, sends emails, updates records, creates documents, etc.
  • - Takes actions independently (or semi-independently) with very little or no constant human supervision
  • - Adapts to changes, learns from outcomes, and completes complex multi-step workflows

    • ### Key characteristics of an agentic employee

  • - Proactive — doesn't just answer questions; it acts to achieve results

  • - **Goal-oriented** — you give it an objective ("prepare the monthly sales report", "onboard this new hire", "resolve this IT ticket") and it figures out how to do it
  • - **Persistent** — many are designed to have memory across days/weeks, behave like a long-term team member
  • - **Role-specific** — often trained or configured for particular jobs (e.g., junior accountant agent, customer support agent, legal research agent, HR coordinator agent)

    • ### Real-world examples (as seen in 2025–2026 products & discussions)

  • - Salesforce Agentforce employee agents — update CRM records, summarize meetings, create cases, handle internal workflows

  • - **Agentic Employee** platforms for law firms — digital workers trained on firm data that draft documents, review contracts, do legal research
  • - HR agents that manage onboarding, shift scheduling, benefits questions, or performance review admin
  • - Finance/ops agents that reconcile accounts, chase approvals, generate reports

    • In short: while a traditional chatbot or copilot **helps** you work, an **agentic employee** is built to **do parts of the job itself** — like hiring a very capable, 24/7 digital teammate that never sleeps and can be scaled infinitely.

    The term became popular around 2025–2026 as companies moved from "AI assistants" to "AI workers" in the so-called **agentic enterprise**.

    Un empleado agentico (también llamado a veces "empleado digital", "agente empleado de IA", "trabajador agentico" o "AI employee") es un sistema de inteligencia artificial autónomo diseñado para funcionar como un trabajador humano dentro de una organización.

    **No es una persona real**, sino un **agente de IA avanzado** que:

  • - Recibe objetivos o tareas

  • - Razona, planifica y descompone las tareas en pasos
  • - Usa herramientas, accede a sistemas/APIs, busca información, envía correos, actualiza registros, crea documentos, etc.
  • - Toma acciones de forma independiente (o semi-independiente) con muy poca o ninguna supervisión humana constante
  • - Se adapta a cambios, aprende de los resultados y completa flujos de trabajo complejos de varios pasos

    • ### Características clave de un empleado agentico

  • - Proactivo — no solo responde preguntas; actúa para lograr resultados

  • - **Orientado a metas** — le das un objetivo ("preparar el informe mensual de ventas", "incorporar a este nuevo empleado", "resolver este ticket de soporte") y él descubre cómo hacerlo
  • - **Persistente** — muchos están diseñados para tener memoria a lo largo de días/semanas y comportarse como un miembro del equipo a largo plazo
  • - **Específico por rol** — suelen estar entrenados o configurados para trabajos concretos (ej.: agente contable junior, agente de atención al cliente, agente de investigación legal, agente coordinador de RRHH)

    • ### Ejemplos reales (vistos en productos y discusiones de 2025–2026)

  • - Agentes empleados de Salesforce Agentforce — actualizan registros en CRM, resumen reuniones, crean casos, manejan flujos internos

  • - Plataformas de **empleados agenticos** para firmas de abogados — trabajadores digitales entrenados con los datos de la firma que redactan documentos, revisan contratos, hacen investigación legal
  • - Agentes de RRHH que gestionan onboarding, programación de turnos, preguntas de beneficios o administración de evaluaciones de desempeño
  • - Agentes de finanzas/operaciones que concilian cuentas, persiguen aprobaciones, generan reportes

    • En resumen: mientras un chatbot tradicional o un copiloto te **ayuda** a trabajar, un **empleado agentico** está construido para **hacer partes del trabajo él mismo** — como contratar a un compañero digital muy capaz, disponible 24/7, que nunca duerme y que se puede escalar infinitamente.

    El término se popularizó alrededor de 2025–2026 cuando las empresas pasaron de "asistentes de IA" a "**trabajadores de IA**" en lo que se llama la **empresa agentica** o **agentic enterprise**.

    ---

    name: fivedbu-manager

    description: Manages local relational databases (DBF, NTX, CDX) and remote databases (ODBC, ADO, MySQL, MariaDB, Access, SQLite) using native FiveDBU functions. Use this skill when you need to explore schemas, create or modify table structures, execute indexes, perform CRUD operations, or do physical cleanup (Pack/Zap).

    ---



    • ## Goal


    Act as the bridge between the user and the FiveDBU platform to manage databases, allowing for CRUD operations, structural modifications, and advanced queries either autonomously or assisted.



    ## How to use it


    When the user requests to operate on a database, map their intention to one of the following FiveDBU capabilities and execute the corresponding commands:



    ### 1. File and Connection Management


  • - **Local Files:** Use the application's functions to open `.dbf` files (including CDX/NTX indexes).

  • - **ODBC/ADO Connections:** To interact with DBASE, FOXPRO, EXCEL, MSACCESS, MSSQL, MYSQL, SQLITE, ORACLE, and POSTGRE data sources.

  • - **Direct Connections:** Establish connections to MySQL or MariaDB servers and list available schemas.


    • ### 2. Record Manipulation (CRUD)


  • - **Add/Edit:** Use the quick edit capability (`FASTEDIT`) or append blank records (`DbAppend`).

  • - **Logical and Physical Deletion:** Mark records for deletion, delete them in bulk by evaluating conditions (`DeleteFor`), or recover them (`Recall`).

  • - **Global Replacement:** Mass update values in specific fields using conditional iterations (`For`/`While`).


    • ### 3. Maintenance and Structure


  • - **Structure (Struct):** Define, add, or alter the table definition (name, type, length, decimals).

  • - **Indexes:** Build index tags defining the natural order, filters (`For`), or mathematical and logical rules (`Unique`, `Descend`).

  • - **Physical Maintenance:** Use `Pack` to purge records marked for deletion, or `Zap` to completely empty a table.

  • - **Import/Export:** Generate a Harbour code transcription (`TxtStruct`) to replicate tables.


    • ### 4. Queries and Environment


  • - **Search and Inspection:** Initiate incremental searches (`Search`) relying on index keys.

  • - **Filters and Queries:** Build and apply local macros (`DbSetFilter`) or use the *Query Builder* to inject SQL clauses into an ADO recordset.

  • - **Workareas:** Gather information from active areas (`Workareas`) including Alias, Record Count, Shared Status, and Paths.


    • ### 5. Integrated Development Tools


  • - **Console and Processes:** Interpret direct FiveDBU commands or execute small dynamic code scripts.

  • - **Hex Editor:** Analyze and edit binaries, `.exe`, `.dll`, or `.dbf` files at the byte level.

  • - **WebApp:** Launch the embedded web server to operate the database remotely.


    • ## Decision Tree


  • - If the user wants to connect to network databases -> Prioritize **ODBC/ADO** connectors.

  • - If the user needs to free up disk space or clean a table -> Suggest **Pack** or **Zap**, but remind them to change the environment to "Exclusive" mode.

  • - If there are file corruption issues -> Use the **Hex Editor**.

  • - If data searching/grouping is needed -> Choose between **Search** (for simple keys) and **Query Builder** (for complex comparison logics).


    • ## Important Rules


  • - The agent must always consider and verify if the environment allows exclusivity (Exclusive) or if it is shared (Shared) depending on the invoked tool.

  • - Act as an expert in xBase/Harbour systems, using precise technical terminology (Alias, RecNo, RDD).

    # Implementation Plan for agentic_fivedbu.prg

    This plan describes the creation of `agentic_fivedbu.prg`, an autonomous, AI-driven database management tool based on the HIX AIOS architecture and FiveDBU features.

    ## Goal Description

    Create an agentic interface for FiveDBU that allows users to interact with their databases (DBF, ADO, MySQL, etc.) using natural language and voice. It will combine the frontend capabilities of [chat.prg](file:///c:/AIOS/chat.prg), the backend reasoning of [aios.prg](file:///c:/AIOS/aios.prg), and the database management features of [fivedbu.prg](file:///c:/AIOS/fivedbu.prg) according to the rules in [agentic_fivedbu_skill.md](file:///c:/AIOS/agentic_fivedbu_skill.md).

    ## Proposed Changes

    ### Core Application

    #### [NEW] agentic_fivedbu.prg

    This will be the main entry point and the core reasoning loop for the database agent.

  • - Initialize the UI/Web Server similar to chat.prg but adapted for database tasks.

  • - Implement the reasoning loop similar to [aios.prg](file:///c:/AIOS/aios.prg) but with a custom `cSystemPrompt` focused on the `fivedbu-manager` persona.
  • - Provide a set of functions (tools) that the AI can call to manage databases:
  • - `db_open` / `db_connect` (Local DBF, ODBC, ADO, MySQL)
  • - `db_query` (Execute SQL or XBase commands)
  • - `db_structure` (Get table structure)
  • - `db_crud` (Append, Edit, Delete records)
  • - `db_maintenance` (Pack, Zap, Index)
  • - Ensure the agent follows the behavior specified in [agentic_fivedbu_skill.md](file:///c:/AIOS/agentic_fivedbu_skill.md).

    • ### Agent Skills

    #### [NEW] .agent\skills\fivedbu\fivedbu_skill.prg

    A new skill module containing the Harbour functions that wrap FiveDBU operations to be called by the AI.

  • - </s>Aios_DbOpen( hArgs )<e>

  • - `Aios_DbQuery( hArgs )`
  • - `Aios_DbStruct( hArgs )`
  • - `Aios_DbDeleteFor( hArgs )`
  • - `Aios_DbPack( hArgs )`

    • #### [MODIFY] aios.prg

    Register the new fivedbu skills in `BuildAiosFunctions()` and `ExecuteAiosSkill()` so the agent reasoning loop can understand and execute database commands.

    #### [MODIFY] chat_script.js (Optional)

    If specific UI features are needed for database rendering (e.g., rendering a grid data structure in the chat), we might need to add specific HTML structure handling. We will try to map table results to Markdown tables first.

    ## Verification Plan

    ### Automated Tests

  • 1. Compile the new </s>agentic_fivedbu.prg<e>.

  • 2. Ensure the embedded web server starts correctly and UI is accessible.
  • 3. Send a test query via the `GeminiCallFC` to check if `fivedbu` functions are correctly registered and triggered.

    • ### Manual Verification

  • 1. Run </s>agentic_fivedbu.exe<e>.

  • 2. Talk/Type to the agent: "Abre la base de datos de ejemplo local y dime qué tablas tiene."
  • 3. Ask the agent to show the structure of a specific table.
  • 4. Tell the agent to add a test record.
  • 5. Ask the agent to delete that record and execute a Pack.
  • 6. Verify all these steps through the agent's responses and by manually checking the database file.

    Dear Antonio,

    One thought that comes to mind:

    This approach assumes that our existing applications are the right foundation to build an agent layer on top of.
    But what if some of our systems were designed in a much simpler era and are heavily UI-driven or tightly coupled internally?
    In that case, would extracting low-level skills still be the right starting point —
    or would it make more sense to rethink parts of the architecture first?

    I’m asking because for some of us, the real question might not be “How do we agentify what we have?”
    but “Is what we have structurally ready for that step?”
    I see the technical value of extracting skills from existing PRGs.
    But from a business perspective, I wonder whether it might be more effective to start from concrete use cases and derive the required skills from there.
    Otherwise we risk building a complete inventory of capabilities without a clearly defined problem to solve.

    I think maybe we are looking at this from two different but complementary realities.

    Your approach seems focused on infrastructure experimentation — making applications agent-callable by extracting low-level skills.

    For some of us, however, the immediate pressure comes from concrete market demands:
    Customers want remote/web access
    Customers want flexible reporting and dynamic queries
    Customers want better tools to acquire and analyze clients

    So our current priority may not be replacing the GUI with a talking agent.

    It may be:
    Architecture modernization
    Stronger reporting capabilities
    Optional AI enhancements on top of existing workflows

    In that sense, AI could help us modernize and extend our systems — without necessarily replacing them.

    I would be very interested in your view on how these two paths converge in the medium term.
