The Harbour language extension project for VS2013 started!!!
Posted: Sun Jul 06, 2014 09:21 PM
Dear friends,

Today we have started it!

After many chats with different developers trying to choose the right one, (my intention was to hire the most expensive one, as he has a 100% completion rate) today something quite magical happened. A developer who hasn't done any project at all at freelancer appeared: sarabjeetsingh20. He asked so little money that I thought it was just going to be a waste of time, but I decided to ask him to perform a test in order to be considered: to adapt http://www.codeproject.com/Articles/33250/Writing-Your-First-Visual-Studio-Language-Service in order to work with VS2013. if you try to open it with VS2013 it fails.

My idea was to use a bitbucket.org GIT repository, and considered to get him as an extra help for the project development as his offer was so cheap. In a little while he came back to me and sent me the MyCLanguageService.zip fixed to work on VS2013!!!

We have been working together two hours or so, and the first results are outstanding! I have created a bitbucket.org repository with all the work done today:
https://bitbucket.org/fivetech/myclanguageservice

Please go to its wiki at https://bitbucket.org/fivetech/myclanguageservice/wiki/Home, follow the steps and you will be able to use the Irony wizard and also to test the MyC example. And this just happened in two hours aprox. Quite amazing!

He is so happy as this is his first project on freelancer.com so he is doing his best and his tech skills and his attitude are both outstanding! I already paid him a milestone for 50 USD, and tomorrow we will continue with its development. I am closely following what he is doing so we all will be able to reproduce it and test it.

So if you want to join this project from its early beginning, please go to the above bitbucket.org project and follow the wiki steps
