Today Patrick Fan has sent me an email, which I reproduce it here: (I hope that you don't mind it, Patrick

Dear Antonio,

> This is Antonio Linares from FiveTech Software. We
> have been reviewing with
> great interest you recent VFW build and I would like
> to set a better
> cooperation between you and us
>
> You can reach me by email (this one) and also on
> gtalk (Google Talk). Surely
> there are a lot of issues that we could talk about.

Thanks for your interest in reviewing the latest VFW,
which is derived from the great work of yours back in
1997. It is always my pleasure to see what I can do to
make it work better with my limited knowledge of
FiveWin. That is the reason for my work on improving
your Visual IDE for the past five and a half years. In
fact, I am using the project manager everyday for
building and maintaining my business software programs
as well as testing new projects, classes and
functions. Thanks for your original idea of building
such an IDE, I find it very efficient for my daily
work and I can hardly imagine how much longer it will
take me to accomplish my task without using it.

Of course, there are a lot of issues in design and
there are a lot of room to improve. At present, I am
only paying attention to those issues that I encounter
in using VFW in my daily work. Those issues are dealt
with or fixed as a first priority using my limited
time.

> My idea is that we share our points of view on VFW.
> From our side we would
> like to offer you all our suggestions and help to
> make VFW a better product.
> I believe that with some little changes in VFW, it

> attention from FiveWin users. If FiveWin users know
> that you and me are
> cooperating, they will be much more interested in
> VFW.
>

Since FiveTech is the creator of VFW, I am glad to
have precious suggestions from you to improve the
product. I will try my best to see whether I am
capable of making those necessary changes to make it
better.

> Also, you are a great FiveWin master, and I am sure
> that we can learn many
> things from each other
>

I am only a long time user of FiveWin since 1.91, not
a master. You, Antonio, the creator of FiveWin and the
one who initiated the Harbour project, should be the
master of masters. Definitely and hopefully, there are
a lot of things that I can learn from you.

> Please let me know if you are interested in this
> cooperation, and if yes,
> lets organize it together! Thanks
>
> best regards,
>
> Antonio Linares

definite answer 'Yes', I must first have to know more
about the details and scope of such cooperation to see
whether I have sufficient time and ability to do so.

Thanks again for your gift of FWH2.4.

best Regards,

Patrick Fan
